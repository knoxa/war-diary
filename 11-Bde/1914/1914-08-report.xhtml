REPORT ON ACTION OF 1/EAST LANCASHIRE REGIMENT OF THE 27th/28th AUGUST, 1914

TNA: WO 95/1486/1, pp 29-30

copy

Fourth Div

In accordance with verbal instructions from G.O.C. 4th Div, I have the honour to bring to notice the action of the 1st Bn East Lancashire Rgt on the 27th - 28th Aug 1914.

This battalion with some detachments from other units of the 11th Infantry Brigade, in their endeavour to rejoin the Headquarters of the 4th Division, from which they had got separated after the battle of the 26th, marched (35) thirty five miles in 26 hours without food other than the little that was procurable from the villages en route, and yet marched into HAM and through HAM whistling and singing about 7 pm on 27th.

After getting some food (there was not much to be got) from supply wagons south of HAM, this battalion immediately marched back 2¼ miles (37¼ in all) and took up an outpost line covering HAM from the North. This outpost line they held all night until relieved by the 3rd Division the next morning.

HAM was at that time the headquarters of the 2nd Army Corps.

This instance of endurance, cheeriness and fine military spirits is the more worthy of record when it is remembered that these men had been marching and fighting without intermission from the time they got out of the train at LE CATEAU at 4 pm on the 24th, that they had held the FONTAINE-AU-PIRE position for 10 hours under heavy fire, that they had been heavily shelled both at LIGNY and in their retirement from that place on 26th, and that further on the 27th at NAUROY they had again been shelled by the pursuing horse artillery.

The principal credit for this meritorious performance is due among the Officers to

Lt Col le MARCHANT

whose example of cheeriness & self-possession, and whose handing of the men was beyond praise.

Lieut & Adjt BELCHIER

who was indefategable in keeping the men together & in making arrangements for such food and rest as was possible to find them.

Among the N.C.Os & men the following are those most worthy of mention.

C.Sgt Maj Burgess, Sergt Pattinson

These N.C.Os were invaluable throughout in controlling and encouraging tired men. Both showed gallantry and coolness when the column was shelled by pursuing Artillery and by their example did much to keep the men together

A Hunter-Weston
Brig Gen'l
Comdg 11th Inf Brig

Bucy
2.10.14
