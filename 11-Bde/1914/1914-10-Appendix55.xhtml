FromEleventh Inf Brig
Place
Time9.5 am

TNA: WO 95/1487/5, p164

Eleventh Brigade

Report on Infantry attack delivered against my left centre yesterday.

It appears that after a heavy bombardment in which the trenches on the North of ST YVES Road and the two which sit astride the road running East from the Estaminet & held by Lieut Trimmer's platoon were all badly damaged. Lieut Trimmer's platoon suffering very heavily. It is thought that few of his men were left when the Infantry arrived about 4 pm. It was apparently against these two trenches that the attack was pressed home & which eventually penetrated the line. When Lt Trimmer's trenches were visited all the men were in them still, all but one being dead, the survivor is badly wounded. They appear to have fought it out to the last. Lt Atchinson & Wade, realizing the danger a penetration on their left would involve, hastily constructed a barricade on the road at the N. end of the cutting which enabled them to mount a Maxim gun and collect a few rifles behind it & thus enfilading the Germans as they crossed the road. The enemy after advancing about 50 yards W. of the road realized their danger & retired, many no doubt taking what cover they could in buildings etc. It is estimated that about 60 Germans got through, but as it took place after dusk numbers were difficult to estimate but this number were actually seen.

The Germans made no attempt to establish themselves in the buildings & when Maj. Prowse entered them they were empty.


G. Perkins, Capt & Adjt. Hampshire Rgt.
