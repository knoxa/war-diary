FromEleventh Inf Brig
Place
Time9.5 am

TNA: WO 95/1487/5, p164

Eleventh Brigade

Report on Infantry attack delivered against my left centre yesterday.

It appears that after a heavy bombardment in which the trenches on the North of ST YVES Road and the two which sit astride the road running East from the Estaminet & held by Lieut Trimmer's platoon were all badly damaged. Lieut Trimmer's platoon suffering very heavily. It is thought that few of his men were left when the Infantry arrived about 4 pm. It was apparently against these two trenches that the attack was pressed home & which eventually penetrated the line. When Lt Trimmer's trenches were visited all the men were in them still, all but one being dead, the survivor is badly wounded. They appear to have fought it out to the last. Lt Atchinson & Wade, realizing the danger a penetration on their left would involve, hastily constructed a barricade on the road at the N. end of the cutting which enabled them to mount a Maxim gun and collect a few rifles behind it & thus enfilading the Germans as they crossed the road. The enemy after advancing about 50 yards W. of the road realized their danger & retired, many no doubt taking what cover they could in buildings etc. It is estimated that about 60 Germans got through, but as it took place after dusk numbers were difficult to estimate but this number were actually seen.

The Germans made no attempt to establish themselves in the buildings & when Maj. Prowse entered them they were empty.


G. Perkins, Capt & Adjt. Hampshire Rgt.

31st October, 1914

SUMMARY OF INFORMATION

1.   There is no change in frontage occupied by the IIIrd Corps. The centre and right have been subjected to some shelling, but no infantry attack has been made on this portion of our line.

About ST. YVES however, the enemy (consisting of a battalion of the 134th Regiment, XIX Saxon Corps) made a determined attack last night and captured the village, but the Somersetshire L.I. under Major Prowse, counter-attacked and drove the enemy out, thus restoring the line.

2.   NORTH OF THE LYS.

The village of MESSINES was very heavily shelled yesterday and a hostile infantry attack developed in the evening from the DOUVE Valley. Our troops however maintained their position in this locality during the night. This morning most of the village was evacuated by our troops, and it is now partly in our hands and partly in the hands of the Germans.

North of MESSINES and between that place and GHELUVELT, the enemy has developed numerous forces and has been making a strong attack ever since yesterday at mid-day in the direction of YPRES. This attack made some progress at first and drove back our advanced troops, but has not been, and is not likely to be successful in acheiving its object, that is, to reach the town of YPRES. Our troops have delivered a counter-attack today on the enemy, but no report has yet been received as to the result.

North of YPRES the enemy has made repeated attacks against the allied troops and has suffered very heavily without making any progress whatever. The Allies near NIEUPORT took 210 prisoners yesterday.

A German aeroplane was brought down yesterday near YPRES.

3.   SOUTH OF THE LYS.

An attack was made on our IInd Corps yesterday and was repulsed without loss.

The Allies have gained a considerable amount of ground and pressed back the German line about ALBERT.

4.   The 48th Reserve Division of the XXIV Reserve Corps is still located in the country just N.W. of FROMELLES.

The 134th Regiment (XIX Saxon Corps) was located last night about ST. YVES. This Corps cannot have moved very far if at all.

The 119th and 125th Regiments of the XIII Corps have been located in today's fighting about MESSINES. Most of the units of this Corps were located last week in the region about FROMELLES and it seems now almost certain that this Corps was pulled out about 4 nights ago and the XXIV Reserve Corps was put in its place. The XIII Corps, or a portion of it, has evidently moved N. of the LYS to the neighbourhood of MESSINES.

Two Bavarian Jager Battalions have been located about MESSINES and ST. YVES. It seems possible that these are the same battalions which were supporting the Bavarian Cavalry Division and have now been thrown into the fight.

The XXV Reserve Corps which is the only one of the newly raised Corps which has not been brought into action in the Western theatre has now been definitely located against the Russians.

5.   In LORRAINE the French have driven the Germans back over the frontier between the Forests of BEZANGE and PARROY.

6.   RUSSIA. The Russian success in POLAND is much greater than was at first believed. Not only have the German troops N. of the R. PILITZA been driven back and defeated, but now it is officially reported that the Austro-German troops have suffered a heavy defeat in a battle which subsequently took place S. of that River, and the Russians by the 28th had reached the line RADON - LODZ. The Austro-German Army was in full retreat to the South-west.

South of the PRZEMYSL the Russians surrounded and annihilated the 38th Honved Division (Austria) near PODBUZ.

Great importance is attached to this victory of the Russians over the Austro-German forces.


(sigd.) J.H. DAVIDSON

Major, G.S., IIIrd Army Corps.
