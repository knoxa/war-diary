FromELEVENTH INF BRIG
Place½ mile S.E. of BAILLY
Time8.30 am

operation Order No 1

Maj. Gen. T D'O Snow CB Comdg 4th Div

30.8.14

1.  The news of the situation tonight is most satisfactory. The enemy's advance has been checked, both against our right & our left. The situation however requires a short strategical retirement on the part of the 4th Div, but the G.O.C. 4th Div knows that the troops in the Div will willingly respond to yet another exceptional call on their morale.

2.  The Div will march in one column. March table is attached. This march table has been issued to some units as a warning order.

3.  The Div will move tommorrow to the line of the River AISNE.

4.  There will be a long halt probably of 2 or 3 hours at 8 am during which troops will be feed & horses watered.

5.  The withdrawal of the outposts will be arranged so that PONT L'EVEQUE is clear by 6 am.

6.  The bridge mentioned in above para will be blown up by R.E. by order of rearguard commander 4th Div.

7.  Refilling point & supply rendezvous have been specially arranged for.

8.  The no. 15 & 2 Fd Amb'ces having rejoined the Div, the A.D.M.S. will detail one to march after the 11th Inf Brig & the other with the rearguard which is will meet at the cross roads ¼ mile east at 6 am.

9.  Div. H.Q. in CARLEPONT MAIRIE will be closed at 5 am & repoen at the same time at the head of the 11th Inf Brig.

[Signed]

Issued at 2.15 am

TO: Eleventh Brigade
Sender's Number: AA 150 Day of Month: Seventh In reply to Number:

GOC wishes you to forward at your earliest convenience a short account of the action of troops under your command at the battle of LIGNY - HAUCOURT - SEVIGNY on 26th August together with a list of officers, warrant officers, NCO's and men who you want to bring to notice for their good service AAA The list should not exceed 15 names and should be arranged in the order of merit which you consider their actions justify AAA In each case a short account of the reason for your recommendation should accompany each individuals name.
