TO: ROURTH DIV.
Sender's Number: B.M. 4 Day of Month: First In reply to Number:

The bearer of this message (Capt O'Mahoney) reports that a force of German Cavalry, strength a division which advanced from the direction of PIERREFONDS towards ROCQUEMONT being repulsed by gun and rifle fire, retired hurriedly in a S.E. or E. direction. AAA I an holding the St SAUVEUR ridge with infantry (2 bns) along the bottom of the hill. As the right of this front line is rather in the air, I am withdrawing it to take up a shore position on the St SAUVEUR ridge, N of BETHISY facing N and E. I have pushed forward one bn in the direction of the firing this morning to occupy the high ground S of BETHISY. AAA My infantry N of St SAUVEUR have been firing at a few of the enemy's patrols. I shall continue to hold the St SAUVEUR position AAA I should add that from what Capt O'Mahoney states there would appear to be no German Infantry in the immediate vicinity. AAA I suggest that this officer's motor would be very valuable for inter-communication between you and me until I leave the St SAUVEUR position.

Operation Order No 1

Chateau of VERBERIE

1-9-14

Reference BEAUVAIS & SOISSIONS sheet.

1.(a)  The 4th Divisopn & 19th Inf Brig will move today in direction of BARON via BULLY.

2.   The march will begin on two roads but the column will join on to one road near RARAY at a point which will be indicated, and continue the march to BARON in one column.

March table attached. Troops to be ready to move at 7am.

3.   The time of starting of the first unit in each column will be notified as soon as orders are received from Army Corps H.Q.

Each unit must ensure being in touch with the unit in front of it on the line of march so that there may be no delay in moving off when the order to march is received.

4.   Div Amm Col will start for BARON at 4 am and the Train at 5 am. 19th Inf Brig Train and Amm Col with march with the 4th Div Train.

5.   The Rear Guard of eastern column on reaching LA BARDE Fe will be joined by the 32 FAB and will then take over the duties of rear guard to whole division.

Cyclist Co. North Irish Horse & 10th Fd Ambce will send representations to 11th Inf Brig by 5 am for orders.

The rearguard of the western column will remain out until rear of its main body has reached neighbourhood RARAY when it will hand over its duties as rearguard to rearguard of eastern column and rejoin its brigade at the first opportunity.

11th Fd Ambce at VILLENEUVE will join the column at RARAY and will march in rear of 10th Fd Ambce.

6.   All men unable to march will be carried by Fd Ambce and not on wagons of units.

7.   Refilling point for 4th Div and 19th Inf Brig will be at ST MARD.

8.   Reports to CHATEAU VERBERIE till march commences, after that to head of western column, and after RARAY at head of Division.

Lt Col, Gen Staff 4th Division

Issued at 2 am
