TO: FOURTH DIV
Sender's Number: Bm 2 Day of Month: Twentyfifth In reply to Number:

Your G.H.4. received AAA. SOM. L.I. are holding the ridge 1 mile N.W. of BRIASTRE and in close touch with the 10th Inf Brig on their left AAA. Am moving RIF BRIG who are now up just N.W. of BRIASTRE and they will block SOLESMES - BRIASTRE road AAA. The HANTS and E. LANCS are holding the ridge 1 mile S.E. of SOLESMES which they have entrenched AAA. Have received a report through an A.S.C. Officer but which is unconfirmed that there is fighting N of SOLESMES AAA. In view of this fact and that the TENTH INF BRIG are in strength N.W. of BRIASTRE, I do not consider it advisable to move the HANTS and E. LANCS until the heads of the SECOND CORPS approach. AAA. I will then these two Bns to a position in reserve N.W. of BRIASTRE in accordance with your orders. AAA My headquarters will therefore remain at BELLE VUE at present.

Fifth div have been ordered to move via ENGLEFONTAINE - CROIX - MONTAY AAA Their division in two Columns through SOLESMES and AMERVAL AAA. TO: Headquarters, 11th Infantry Brigade
Sender's Number: Day of Month: Twentyfifth In reply to Number:

Report disposition Battalion as follows:-

Hdqrs: In Quievy - BRIASTRE ROAD at 2nd E of TERTRE FM.

Remainder of Battalion:-

2 coys on ridge running north of TERTRE FM facing EAST.

1 coy on same ridge South of TERTRE FM.

1 coy South & parallel to the BRIASTRE - QUIEVY ROAD facing North.

I am in touch with 10th Brigade on my left. The Irish Fusiliers are holding point 119 and Dublin Fusiliers ground to East of it. Seaforth Highlanders are holding a position covering village of QUIEVY. I saw General Haldane and reported to him my position.

An ASC Officer informed me that he could go through SOLESMES in a northerly direction; as the British Rear Guard was holding the northern edge of the village closely pressed by the Germans. I have not been able to verify this statement, but it would appear that the present dispositions do not block the SOLESMES - BRIASTRES ROAD.
